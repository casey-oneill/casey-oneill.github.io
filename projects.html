Roguelike
My first video game

A project undertaken to learn about game development and animation, inspired by games like Enter the Gungeon. This is a 2D roguelike created using the Godot game engine. I began this project during my 2nd year of university, and was able to implement basic combat mechanics and level generation. All assets used in this game are my own creation.

Not seen in the demo are enemies, which use a rudimentary pathfinding algorithm to navigate towards the player.

Concepts Practised
  • Game design
  • 2D Pixel animation
  • Pathfinding with the Godot engine
  • Procedurally generating tile-based maps
  • Game development with the Godot engine
Technology Stack
  • Godot
  • GDScript (similar to Python)

Self-Directed Research Project
Studying Timer-Based Mechanics in Video Games

Under the supervision of Dr. Daniel Rea, Co-Director of the UNB HCI Lab, I am working to complete a self-directed study on the impact of timer-based mechanics in video games. I am currently working to create a implementations of various Choose Your Own Adventure stories using the Twine game engine. Timer-based mechanics will be added semi-randomly, and participants of the study will respond to surveys regarding their experience of this game.

  • Designing a reasearch study
